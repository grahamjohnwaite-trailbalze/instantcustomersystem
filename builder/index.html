Two-Engine Rule

Spotlight Local Mode

Broad local publications such as Peterborough Spotlight, Cambridgeshire Spotlight and Norfolk Spotlight.

  • Usually 25 slots plus outro
  • 80% reusable local-life problems
  • 20% current/local heat
  • Unfiltered, specific and reader-led
  • 6–8 authority lanes and 6–8 activation lanes where natural

Niche Mode

Category newsletters such as Taste Trail, Local Pet Insider and Home Seller Insider.

  • Usually 10–15 slots
  • 3–5 expert/authority articles
  • 3–5 activations
  • Rest audience attention
  • Designed to roll into any city in minutes with local proof swapped in

Master Quality Rules

Non-negotiables

  • Answers first, reader prompts second.
  • Specific local proof, not place-name scattering.
  • No filler to reach slot count.
  • One main treatment per subject per issue.
  • Archive/topic memory before drafting.
  • Fictional reader names once per issue only, and varied across same-week PBS/CBS/NS.
  • Unfiltered edge: say the awkward bit, fairly.
  • Social posts and image ideas are part of the build, not afterthoughts.
  • Commercial fit is hidden inside reader-first editorial.
  • Reader interaction is mandatory: Facebook comments, Messenger replies, nominations, polls and business enquiries beat email replies.
  • Each issue needs local conversation texture: 8–12 ordinary reader-style lines, never invented expert/business quotes.
  • Language must sound like people. Avoid AI-sounding filler and clichés. The word “snacks” may appear no more than once per issue.
